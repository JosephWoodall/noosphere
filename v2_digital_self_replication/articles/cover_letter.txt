What the paper contributes.

Standard neural decoders for prosthetic BCI treat motor intent classification as a static,
per-window task. They have no internal model of how brain state evolves after a motor command is
issued, and they impose an irreducible decoding latency that continuous-DOF control cannot afford.
This paper introduces the Action-Conditioned ZOH-SSM World Model: a bilinear Zero-Order-Hold
State Space encoder whose first recurrent block receives the previous motor command as a
SiLU-gated control input, converting a standard EEG encoder into a genuine world model. A hybrid
CEM+gradient MPC planner exploits this world model for latency-minimising closed-loop control.

The central empirical finding is that static classification accuracy is bounded by labelled data
volume, not by training objective or architecture — both EEGNet and the JEPA encoder sit
approximately 22 percentage points below CSP+LDA across two independent datasets — but that
action-conditioning in the SSM recurrence converts a non-functional decoder (0% closed-loop
convergence) into one competitive with the classical spatial-filter baseline (33.8% vs. 26.3%,
p=0.21). This operationalises the world-model contribution independently of the static
classification bottleneck.

How this submission addresses the prior editorial feedback.

The reviewing editor identified the following specific gaps. Each is resolved in the present
manuscript:

- Overclaiming (state-of-the-art, foundation-model, safety-critical): The new paper makes no
  such claims. The abstract explicitly states that neural methods fall approximately 22 pp below
  CSP+LDA, and Section IV-B directly demonstrates that JEPA pretraining provides no statistically
  detectable benefit over random initialisation at this data scale (p=0.70).

- Leakage-safe protocol detail: Section III-G.2 describes the per-fold isolation mechanism in
  full, including the use of tempfile.TemporaryDirectory() to prevent checkpoint leakage.

- Per-subject statistics: Tables IV and VI provide per-subject balanced accuracy for all 20
  PhysioNetMI subjects and all 9 BCI2a subjects across all primary conditions, including EEGNet
  and EEGConformer per-subject breakdowns.

- Ablations: Three ablations are reported: (1) randomly-initialised encoder (ablates JEPA
  contribution); (2) MLP transition model vs. AC-SSM (isolates the architectural contribution of
  moving action conditioning into the recurrence); (3) CNS cross-modal pretraining.

- Broader and modern baselines: EEGNet, EEGConformer (Song et al., TNSRE 2022), CSP+LDA,
  Riemannian MDM, JEPA encoder, and random-init encoder are all evaluated under the same
  leakage-safe LOSO harness.

- External validation: Section IV-E reports full results on BCI Competition IV Dataset 2a (9
  subjects, 4-class MI), confirming that the approximately 22 pp CSP–neural gap is structural and
  dataset-independent.

- Calibration metrics: Section IV-F quantifies decoder uncertainty calibration (r=0.088,
  r^2<0.01, N=1,277 trials) and explicitly identifies miscalibration as a limitation.

- Confidence intervals and p-values: Bootstrap 95% CIs (10,000 samples) appear in all seven
  result tables; Wilcoxon signed-rank tests with full p-values are reported for eleven pairwise
  comparisons in Table II.

- Data governance: Section III-G.1 provides the PhysioNet dataset record identifier, Creative
  Commons Attribution 4.0 licence statement, and an explicit statement that no institutional
  ethics review is required for secondary analysis of this fully de-identified public dataset.

- Alignment-window sensitivity: Table III repeats the primary evaluation across three epoch
  windows (2 s, 4 s, 2.5 s), demonstrating that the CSP–neural gap is stable at 20–24 pp
  regardless of alignment choice.

JBHI fit.

The paper targets a concrete unmet clinical need (responsive continuous-DOF prosthetic control
for the approximately 2.1 million US upper-limb amputees) and contributes a quantified
architectural solution evaluated under a rigorous, reproducible protocol. The biomedical
relevance is direct: the data-limited bottleneck finding has immediate implications for how
future BCI decoders should be designed and evaluated, and the closed-loop simulation provides an
operationalised evaluation framework for world-model BCI research.
