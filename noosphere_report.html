Key finding: The Noosphere S4 EEG Encoder achieves 92.9% within-subject accuracy (after brief calibration) and 39.9% zero-shot accuracy on unseen users across 5 public motor-imagery datasets. S4 outperforms the standard CSP+LDA baseline by +51.0pp within-subject and -2.3pp cross-subject. 5 of 5 datasets reach Wilcoxon significance (p<0.05, paired per-subject). Mean Cohen's κ = 0.897 and AUC-ROC = 0.985.

Methodology

ItemDetail
EEG input C3/Cz/C4 selected by MNE channel name; z-scored per trial; resampled to 256 Hz; 1-second segments (256 samples)
S4 encoder S4EEGEncoder: 4× S4Block (d_model=128), Dirichlet evidential head, TDA topology (if ripser available)
Pretraining Trunk pretrained on pooled subjects: 80 epochs, AdamW, cosine LR (warmup 10 ep), label smoothing ε=0.1, per-class weights, grad clip 1.0
Fine-tuning Head only (trunk frozen): 150 ep max, patience=20 early stop, 15% stratified val split, best checkpoint restored
Calibration Post-hoc temperature scaling T=1.5 before inference
CSP+LDA baseline OVR CSP (2 filters/class, GEVD), log-variance features, regularised LDA (Ledoit-Wolf λ=0.1). Same splits as S4.
Within-subject Chronological 75/25 per-class split. No leakage. “After brief calibration” scenario.
LOSO Leave-one-subject-out. Train on N-1, evaluate held-out zero-shot. “New user, no calibration” scenario.
Statistics Wilcoxon signed-rank (paired per-subject, two-sided). Effect size r = |Z|/√N. Threshold p<0.05.
Datasets MOABB public benchmarks: BNCI2014-001, BNCI2014-004, Schirrmeister2017, PhysionetMI, Cho2017.
